It was a normal day at high school, and we all gathered for lunch
A girl sat beside her friends, just about to munch
All of sudden there was shouting, things started to get loud
Students were gathering around the girl, forming a crowd
She was getting beat up, in front of the entire school
All because one day, people decided she was no longer cool


I wasn’t that cool either, as far as I could see
This made me wonder, if the next ambush was going to be me
But I wasn’t going to find out, if I was accepted or denied
I went around school, looking for a place to hide
That’s when I found, art class was open every recess
It became my refuge, my outlet, it was my creative nest
I know my drawing skills weren’t as awesome as my peers
yet I continued anyways, for the next few years

I wanted to increase my skills and knowledge
So after high school, I went straight to college
The artists there were skilled and competitive
I got discouraged, and felt very negative
and as time passed, I could not help but notice
my classmates were growing, and I was still a novice

I decided I should do something more purposeful with my life
I quit art for good, became a mother, and a housewife
I loved my new journey, no need to look back
I had everything I needed, there is nothing I lack
For years I completely stopped making art
No time or interest, I let it depart

One day I noticed, something about my kid’s school
There were no sports, field trips, music, - this was boring and cruel
Parents wanted something educational and fun
But the principal apologized, because funding there was none

So I gathered cheap supplies, and filled up my cart
And decided afterschool, these students were going to do art
I would teach them myself, as much as I knew
And little by little my art class grew
I saw in these students, something I once saw in me
A creative mind ready and wanting to be free

My intentions were originally to just teach the student
But during class time I had a moment
I remembered how much I used loved to create
When it did not even matter, if the result was great
But somehow I quit doing what I loved to do
I had too much doubt, and encouragement - few

I wanted to give this art thing another shot
But teaching I’ll do, selling artwork I will not
now I decided I’m not going to judge or compare myself
Success cannot be measured by likes or wealth
I want to inspire young people I come across
Because I know it is easy for kids to become loss
I know there is someone out there like me
That needs a special place for them to be
A place they can isolate, focus, and create
To have a joyful therapeutic state

Although I am a teacher, I am still an artist
But not like the ones, common among us
My material is no longer just paint and canvas
It is the students I train, in me they put their trust
I shape my students minds, like a sculpture does clay
I carve out the negativity,and let the good things stay
I like to give young people what I didn’t have
To help them succeed makes me glad
